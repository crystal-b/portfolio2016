Klotz Watches + Clocks

Website redesign for a timepiece retailer in San Francisco

Brief
This project involved planning a redesign of a local business website for my Information Design class.

Process
Information architecture, card sorting, wireframes. I wrote user scenarios to help figure out why a customer would visit the site and what information they would want to find. Each of these important pages and every step of the interactive pages needed wireframes, essentially webpage blueprints, to help organize the information on each page.

Research
The original website had a confusing structure including two different homepages. As part of the planning process, I cataloged all of the current website pages, determined which ones were unnecessary, added and consolidated pages as needed, diagrammed workflows of engaging interactive elements like allowing users to explore watchmakers on a world map. The original site also had a forum that was old an inactive, but gave me an idea for making the site engaging. I made some wireframes where users could upload stories about timepieces and makers from around the world.

Prototype
Creating a clickable prototype with Fireworks was simple enough for initial testing. I wanted the add a story feature to work on mobile though and needed a responsive prototype for testing across devices. Using ZURB's Foundation framework for the final prototype was the answer.
